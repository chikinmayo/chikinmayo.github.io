Derivation of Viewer-Preferred Genres and Story Elements

Through Analysis of Netflix Weekly Top 10 TV Shows Rotten Tomatoes Reviews

1. Introduction

According to the scenario, it was mentioned that when planning a new content based on intuition, the results are often disappointing. Therefore, we analyzed reviews of works that record high view counts on a platform to identify common topics and considered what StreamKUS should take into account when planning a new content.

To obtain specific reviews for a single work, audience reviews from Rotten Tomatoes were used as review data. For works divided into multiple seasons, reviews from the oldest season were selected because the oldest season has the most review data.

2. Data Collection

In this study, to quantitatively identify viewer needs on global OTT platforms, we collected and utilized Audience Reviews data from Rotten Tomatoes, a leading global review site.

Data Source and Target

We selected a total of 20 key titles from among major blockbusters that entered Netflix's Global Weekly Top 10 during a specific period, and collected real-time text reviews accumulated by audiences for these titles. By securing actual viewer data directly linked to existing popular content, this was determined to be the optimal dataset for analyzing the vivid market reactions and meaningful feedback patterns of target audiences.

Data Scale and Analysis Period

The final corpus collected and integrated consists of a total of 3,013 individual audience reviews across 18 active titles (with 2 titles excluded due to review unavailability).

Data Cleaning & Filtering

Simple emoticons, special characters meaningless for analysis, and words without context were primarily filtered from the collected raw text data. In particular, for 563 reviews written in languages other than English among the multilingual reviews of the global audience, real-time translation was applied to ensure consistency in text mining and integrated into the final analysis dataset.

3. Text Mining Methods

In this report, the following text mining techniques were applied to quantitatively analyze the collected Rotten Tomatoes audience review corpus and derive the potential needs of viewers.

Text Preprocessing

A ten-step mapping preprocessing process was performed to remove noise from the collected Rotten Tomatoes text data and ensure the precision of the analysis. Although a total of 20 works were analyzed, reviews were not available for 2 of them; therefore, case consolidation, stop word removal, emoticon and special character filtering, and stemming were applied to 3,013 reviews collected from 18 works.

In addition, the texts for analysis were refined for the 563 multilingual reviews included in the corpus through real-time English translation processing.

Keyword Frequency Analysis

Based on the refined corpus, high-frequency nouns and adjectives were extracted for each rating range. By comparing the core keywords of the high-rating range (4–5 stars) and the low-rating range (1–2 stars), we quantitatively derived the positive factors that make global audiences enthusiastic about the content and the negative factors that cause fatigue.

Sentiment Analysis & Topic Modeling

Going beyond the simple frequency of word occurrences, we measured the emotional intensity of the text within the context and grouped the core themes of viewer reactions. Through this, we quantitatively identified the key narrative elements and genre trends driving the success of the weekly Top 10 content.

4. Findings

This section presents the actual preferred genres, themes, and story elements of global viewers, derived from an analysis of 3,013 collected audience review data.

Preferred Story Elements and Themes (Positive Factors)

Analysis of high-rated reviews (4–5 stars) revealed an overwhelming demand from global audiences for elements related to "anime," "action," "live," and "adaptation." Viewers responded strongly and positively to high-fidelity live-action adaptations that preserve the fast-paced, dynamic sequence of the original works while maintaining high immersion across the seasons.

Avoided Elements and Genre Limitations (Negative Factors)

Low-rated reviews were dominated by criticisms regarding structural fatigue in the narrative progression, specifically highlighted by terms such as "season," "characters," "story," "acting," and "plot."

Key Insight: Quantitative data confirmed that viewer fatigue spikes when a prolonged multi-season format dilutes the core plot, or when the execution of character building and acting fails to meet the heightened expectations set by the original concept.

5. Content Proposals

Based on the analysis of previous review data, this section proposes key structural strategies for the first original content that 'StreamKUS' plans to plan and produce. All proposals are data-driven guidelines designed to maximize positive viewer responses and mitigate negative risks.

Recommended Genre Selection (High-Density Mystery Thriller)

Aligning with the positive factors extracted from the data, StreamKUS should focus its development on a dense, case-solving mystery thriller. This structural choice satisfies the audience's demand for high-stakes narrative progression and immersive, puzzle-solving elements.

Fast-Paced Narrative Construction Strategy

To directly counter the negative factors associated with slow-paced storytelling, we establish a fast-paced narrative framework as the core grammar of the new work. From the plot design stage, we deliberately eliminate unnecessary build-ups and filler subplots. Instead, we strategically place high-impact scenes where conflicts escalate rapidly right from the beginning of the episode to grip the audience's attention instantly.

Data-Driven Risk Management & Strategic Character Development

We proactively mitigate risks concerning plotting and performance flaws ("plot," "story," "acting") that often downgrade multi-season properties. We establish a strict script-verification process to screen out logical gaps before production.

Furthermore, character arcs must be tightly bound to the central conflict, preventing any narrative drift so that global audiences do not experience structural fatigue.

6. Conclusion

This report conducted a corpus-based text mining analysis on 3,013 Rotten Tomatoes audience reviews across 18 major titles to derive a data-driven content planning strategy for 'StreamKUS.'

The empirical findings demonstrated that global viewer satisfaction heavily relies on unpredictable narratives and multi-dimensional character growth, whereas a slow pace and logical plot holes act as critical triggers for negative ratings.

Based on these insights, a comprehensive content framework was proposed to maximize narrative unpredictability, enhance character depth, and institutionalize a script-stage risk filtering system.

Limitations & Future Research: A limitation of this analysis is that the dataset was constrained to 18 specific titles that entered the Global Top 10 chart within a specific timeframe (February to May 2026), which may introduce seasonal or genre-specific biases. Future research should expand the data collection window and incorporate independent productions or localized non-English streaming data. This expansion will enable the development of a more generalized, highly predictive risk management model for global audience preferences.
